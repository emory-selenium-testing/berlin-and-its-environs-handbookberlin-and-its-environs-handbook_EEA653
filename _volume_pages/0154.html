{% raw %}To the S. of the Linden lies the Friedrich-Stadt (p. 47), the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}most regularly built quarter of Berlin. It is intersected from N.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to S. by the Friedrich-Strasse, by the Wilhelm-Strasse farther{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the W., and by the Charlotten-Strasse and Markgrafen-Strasse{% endraw %}
{% raw %}farther to the E.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Friedrich-Strasse (PI. R, 24-22, G, 22) is the longest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}street in the inner town. Including the section to the N. of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Linden (p. 144), it measures 2 M. from the former Oranienburg Gate{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the former Halle Gate, which lies iy4 M. to the S. of the Linden.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The central portion, near the Linden, is flanked on both sides with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}handsome and substantial business-houses, including the retail pre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mises of several important breweries (comp. p. 9).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the corner of the Friedrich-Str. and the Behren-Str., and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}opposite the S. entrance to the Kaiser-Galerie (p. 56), is the build¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing of the Pschorr Brewery, built by Kayser & Von Groszheim, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}decorated with paintings by M. Koch and Flashar. On the upper floor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is Castan's Panopticum (p. 38).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Behren-Strasse (PI. R, 22), the first cross-street (running{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E. and W.), contains many handsome modern buildings, several of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which were erected by large banking houses. To the E. of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Friedrich-Strasse, on the S. side, are Nos. 38-39, the Dresdner{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bank (p. 34), built by Heim; No. 32, the Berliner Handels-Gesell¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}schaft, built by Messel; No. 47, the Haus Trarbach (wine restaur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ant , see p. 8), built by Walther in a modern Romanesque style.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}(comp. p. 34), built by Heim; and No. 46, the Berliner Bank. To{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the W. of the Friedrich-Strasse, Nos. 55-57, on the N. side, is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Metropol Theatre (p. 28), built by Fellner & Hellmer (passage to{% endraw %}
