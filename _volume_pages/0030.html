{% raw %}e. Stadtbahn and Ringbahn. Electric Elevated and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Underground Railway.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Comp. Plans opposite the title-page and at p. 13, and the Map at p. 185.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Stadtbahn, or City Railway, built in 1874-82, is 10 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in length and stretches through the N. half of the inner city from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Westend-Charlottenburg on the W. to Stralau-Rummelsburg on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E. It is connected with the Ringbahn, built in 1867-77, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}runs through the N. suburbs and makes a wide sweep round the S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}portion of the city. Trains run on the Stadtbahn from about 5 a.m.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}till after midnight at intervals of 2-5 minutes, and on the Ring¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bahn every 10-20 minutes. There are only second and third class{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carriages. Fares for any 5 stations 15 and 10 pf. (monthly tickets{% endraw %}
{% raw %}472 and 3 Jl), beyond that distance 30 and 20 pf. (monthly tickets{% endraw %}
{% raw %}7 and 472 Jl)- No time should be lost in taking seats, as the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stoppages are extremely brief. Notice-boards indicate the point at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which second-class carriages come to a stand, the name of the sta¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion, and the direction in which the trains run. Passengers accident¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ally carried past their destination may return thither without extra{% endraw %}
{% raw %}charge on applying at once to the station-master.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Stadtbahn has four tracks, of which the two northern¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}most are used for the intramural traffic and by the Ringbahn trains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(see below), while the two southernmost are traversed by main¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}line trains (see p. 1; special entrances). All four tracks are used{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the suburban trains. The stations are (from W. to E.): Char¬{% endraw %}
