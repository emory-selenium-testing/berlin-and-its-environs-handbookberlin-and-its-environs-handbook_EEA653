{% raw %}Town Palace. POTSDAM. Section 14. 191{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Railway Station is situated in the Teltow Suburb, on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left (E.) bank of the Havel. The Lunge Briicke, which leads{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thence to the town, rests partly on the W. end of the 'Freundschafts-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Insel' and is adorned with eight typical figures of soldiers since the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}time of the Great Elector, by Herter (1895). — On the island, close to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the bridge, is an Equestrian Statue of Emperor "William I.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Herter, erected in 1900. A goddess of victory is seated in front{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the pedestal, which is embellished with reliefs of Prince William{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as 'galloper' at Bar-sur-Aube in 1814 and of the Entry into Paris{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1871. From beside the monument we command a view, to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S., of the Brauhausberg, with the Military Academy, while from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the bridge we have a view, to the E., of the Church of the Holy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ghost and Babelsberg.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the right bank, in the middle of the street, stands the lime-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tree where petitioners used to station themselves in order to attract{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the attention of Frederick the Great, when he was in residence at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Town Palace.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *Town Palace, or Studt-Schloss, originally erected about{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1600, but dating in its present form from its reconstruction by Kno¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}belsdorff in 1745-51, is a remarkable specimen of the dignified classic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}style, though built at the zenith of the rococo period. The excellent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}materials used and the admirable taste displayed in the execution{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the interior place it on a very high level. Only the apartments{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the groundfloor of the S. main building are shown (adm., see p.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}191; tickets at the N.E. angle of the court).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the S.E. corner Frederick the Great's richly decorated apartments,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with sumptuous furniture and noteworthy pictures by Pater, Lancret, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pesne, have been preserved in their original condition. Adjoining the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Library, which is separated from the Bedroom by a massive silver{% endraw %}
{% raw %}balustrade only, is a Cabinet with double doors, from which the dining-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}table could be let down by means of a trap-door, and where the king{% endraw %}
{% raw %}occasionally dined with his friends without risk of being overheard by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his attendants. The Study in the S.E. angle is noteworthy. (The library{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and bedroom are still frequently used for royal christenings.) — In the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}centre is the Marble Saloon, built by the Great Elector and richly decor¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ated by Frederick the Great. It contains a ceiling-painting, four large{% endraw %}
{% raw %}allegorical paintings, and three bronze reliefs, referring to the achieve¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ments of the Great Elector. The adjoining *Bronze Saloon, with its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}white panelled walls and mirrors adorned with charming bronze-gilt{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ornaments by J. M. Rambly and Schwitzer, is an admirable example of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rococo decoration. Over the fireplace is a double portrait of King Frederick{% endraw %}
{% raw %}William I. and Augustus the Strong, by Silvestre. The handsome French{% endraw %}
{% raw %}clock opposite should be noticed. — Farther on are the Reception Rooms,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}containing paintings by Hasenpflug, Begas, Gudin, etc., and marble groups{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by F. Wolff and Moller. — In the S.W. corner is the so-called Russian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Suite, used for distinguished guests.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In front of the palace, on the bank of the Havel, extends the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lustgarten, with a colonnade and fountain, decorated with statues{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the early 18th century. Near the fountain are bronze busts of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}personages eminent at the period of the war of liberation, by Ruuch.{% endraw %}
