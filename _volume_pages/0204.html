{% raw %}164 Section 9. BERLIN. Gnaden-Kirche.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Geological Institution and School of Mines (PL{% endraw %}
{% raw %}R, 21; plan, p. 162) was built in 1874-78. The School of Mines, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was founded in 1860 and has now about 100 students, occupies the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}groundfloor. In the vestibule are two paintings by L. Spangenberg{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(the Kurische Nehrung, and the Papenkaul near Gerolstein). The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}glass-covered court and the gallery of the first floor are occupied{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the Museum of Mining and Smelting (adm., see p. 38). The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}processes of extracting and working the ore are shown in the court.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We may also notice here the gigantic fossil tree-trunks and the fine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}examples of artistic casting from the former royal foundry at Berlin.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the gallery of the first floor is a systematic collection of minerals{% endraw %}
{% raw %}obtained by mining; in the N.E. corner a room containing the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mineralogical collection. — The other rooms of the first floor are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}occupied by the Prussian Museum of National Geology. — On{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the second floor (no adm.) are the offices of the Geological Institute{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and a valuable collection of amber from Kbnigsberg.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Invaliden-Str., to the W. of the School of Mines, rises{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the *Gnaden- Kirche (PI. R, 21; open daily, 12-1 and 5-7),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a Romanesque sandstone building by M. Spitta (A. 1902), erected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1891-95 to the memory of the Empress Augusta. The elaborate{% endraw %}
{% raw %}choir has stained-glass windows by Linnemann and mosaics designed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Geselschap. In front of the church stands an obelisk commem¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}orating the loss of the corvette Amazon.e in 1861. — Farther on,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the W. of the Scharnhorst-Str., a new building for the Kaiser{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Wilhelm Academy is being erected (comp. p. 145).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Invalidenhaus (PL R, 21), on the W. side of the Scharn¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}horst-Str., erected by Frederick the Great in 1748 'lseso et invicto{% endraw %}
{% raw %}militi', is devoid of architectural interest. Opposite, in the In-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}validen-Park, is the National Wurriors' Monument, a Corinthian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}column of iron, 145 ft. high, erected in 1854 to the memory of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}soldiers who fell in 1848-49 (view from the top; apply to porter of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Invalidenhaus; adm. 10 pf.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Invaliden-Kirchhof, adjoining the Invalidenhaus on the N., is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the burial-place of many distinguished officers, including Scharnhorst{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(d. 1813; monument by Schinkel, with reliefs by Tieck); Boyen (d. 1848),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}founder of the 'Landwehr'; Friesen (d. 1814); Winterfeldt (d. 1757;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}transferred hither in 1857); and Tauenzien von Wittenberg (d. 1824).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the W. of the Invaliden-Park the Invaliden-Strasse crosses{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Berlin und Spandau Canal, which is connected with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Spree on the S. by means of the Humboldt-Hafen (PL R, 21) and{% endraw %}
