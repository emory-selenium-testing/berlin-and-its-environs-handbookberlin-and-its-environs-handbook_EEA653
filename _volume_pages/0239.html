{% raw %}Friedens-Kirche. POTSDAM. Section 14. 193{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tor, erected by Fred. William IV. in 1852 in honour of the return{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of his brother Prince William (afterwards Emp. William I.) from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the campaign in Baden (1849). A few minutes farther a path di¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}verging to the right from the Nauen road and passing through{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pretty grounds leads in l/2 hr. to the Neue Garten (p. 197).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The usual entrance, however, to the Park of Sanssouci is by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 'Green Railing', reached by an avenue bearing a little to the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the Brandenburg Gate. On the right, before the gate-house{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(tickets for the Mausoleum, see below), rises the *Priedens-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Kirche, or Church of Peace, in the early-Christian basilica style,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}designed by Persius, and completed in 1850, the favourite building{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Fred. William IV. The detached campanile is 130 ft. in height.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Atrium (the 'paradise' of the ancient basilicas), in front of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the church, stand Rauch's *Group of Moses, Aaron, and Hur, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(in the centre) a copy of Thorvaldsen's Risen Christ. To the S. of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the atrium are cloisters, the entrance to which from the park is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}formed by a terracotta reproduction of the entrance to the abbey-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church of Heilsbronn. In the cloisters are a few fragments of early-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Christian sculptures.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The somewhat bare Interior of the basilica, borne by sixteen Ionic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns in black marble, contains, in front of the chancel, the burial-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vaults of Frederick William IV. (d. 1861) and his Queen Elizabeth (d. 1873).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The apse is adorned with an old Venetian mosaic from the church of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}San Cipriano di Malamocco, representing Christ, Mary, and Peter on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right, and John the Baptist and St. Cyprian on the left.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the N. side of the atrium is the Mausoleum of Emp.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Frederick 111. (adm., see p. 189; tickets in the gate-house, to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right), erected by Raschdorff in 1890 in imitation of the chapel of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Innichen in Tyrol. The interior of the circular domed structure is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}supported by nine columns of labradorite. The marble *Sarcophagi{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the emperor (d. 1888) and of Empress Victoria (d. 1901) as well{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as those of their sons Waldemar and Sigismund are by R. Begas.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the altar-niche is a *Pieta by Rietschel (1845). The dome is em¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bellished with mosaics of angels, after Ewald's designs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Entering the *Park of Sanssouci by the green gate and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bearing to the right round a corner we soon reach a reduced marble{% endraw %}
{% raw %}replica of Rauch's equestrian statue of Frederick the Great (p. 57).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beyond it is the Great Fountain (p. 189), the water of which rises{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to a height of 130 ft. The twelve figures surrounding the basin are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}French works of the 18th century. The Mercury and the Venus (co¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pies; originals see p. 100), by Pigalle, were presented by Louis XV.;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the figures of Hunting and Fishing are by Lambert Sigisbert Adam,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and most of the others are by his brother Francois Gaspard Adam.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the N., in front of the fountain, is the porphyry bust of Duke{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Paolo Jordanzio of Bracciano, bought by Frederick the Great for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}3000Z. The fountain is situated in the broad main avenue, l'/4 M.{% endraw %}
