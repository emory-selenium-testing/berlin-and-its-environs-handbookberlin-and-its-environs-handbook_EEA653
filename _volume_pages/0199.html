{% raw %}The Wilhelm-Str. (p. 122) is continued to the N. of the Linden{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the Neue Wilhelm-Strasse (PL R, 20). In the latter, at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}corner of the Dorotheen-Str. (p. 144), is the Militury Academy,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}founded by Scharnhorst in 1810 for German officers. The present{% endraw %}
{% raw %}building, by Bernhardt & Schwechten, dates from 1882. Farther to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the N. are the Physiological, Second Chemical, and Physical{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Institutes of the University.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beyond the Marschalls-Brucke the Luisen-Strasse (PL R, 21,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}20) runs parallel with the Friedrich-Strasse to the Neue Tor. Near{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by to the left, Schiffbauer-Damm 25, is the Neue. Operetten-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Theater, by W. Hentschel (1908). At Luisen-Str. 32 is an annexe{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Imperial Home Office (comp. p. 123). A tablet on No. 24a,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}opposite, marks the house where the historian Ranke died in 1886{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(comp. p. 167). No. 35 is the office of the Berlin Electricity Works,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which produce the force for the electric tramways, the lighting of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}streets, etc.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Farther on, at the corner of the Schumann-Str. and the Luisen-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Str., is a monument to Albrecht von Graefe, the oculist (d. 1870),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with excellent reliefs in terracotta, by Siemering. Near this point{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the Charite' (PL R, 21), a large hospital founded in 1710, now{% endraw %}
{% raw %}being rebuilt. The Charite serves also as a teaching institution in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}connection with the University (11 clinics), and is united with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pathological Institute, which reached its present importance under{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the management of Prof. Virchow (d. 1902). Visitors are admitted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the hospital on Wed., Sat., & Sun., 2-3 p.m.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At No. 56 Luisen-Str. is the Veterinary College (PI. R, 21;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}400 students), opened in 1790. In front of the main building is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bronze statue of A C. Gerluch(A. 1877), a former rector, by Panzner.{% endraw %}
