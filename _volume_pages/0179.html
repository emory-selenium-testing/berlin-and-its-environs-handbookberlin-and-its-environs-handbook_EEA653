{% raw %}Park (PI. R, 17), or Exhibition Park, which is intersected by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Stadtbahn. It is accessible also from the Invaliden-Str. and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the Lehrte Station of the Stadtbahn (tramways, see below).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}An annual exhibition called the Grosse Berliner Kunstuusstellung{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is held here in summer (adm., see p. 37; catalogue 1 Jl, with illus¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trations 2 Jl; concerts, see p. 30). The Domed Hall in which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the exhibitions take place is a sumptuous baroque erection, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sculptured groups at the corners, by Hundrieser, Eberlein, Geiger,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Kaffsack. The ceiling-paintings are by W. Friedrich. — In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the W. part of the park (main entrance at Invaliden-Str. 57) is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}restaurant and the Urania Observatory (adm., see p. 42; comp.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}p. 121).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tramways (pp. 15-23): Nos. 4, 6, 7, 9, 12-15, 18, 23, 21 (Alt-Moabit),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Nos. 2, 10, 11, 16, 19, Q, V (Invaliden-Str.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the S. of the Konigs-Platz the broad *Sieges-Allee (PL{% endraw %}
{% raw %}R, 20, 19), or Avenue of Victory, runs through the E. part of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tiergarten, adorned in 1898-1901, at the expense of the Emperor,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with 32 marble Statues of Prussian Rulers. Behind each mon¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}arch is a semicircular marble bench, adorned in the style prevalent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}during his reign, and bearing hermes-busts of two eminent con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temporaries. The statues of the E. row only are portraits.{% endraw %}
