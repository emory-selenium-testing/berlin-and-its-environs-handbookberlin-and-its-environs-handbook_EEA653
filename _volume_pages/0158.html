{% raw %}to the W. At the corner (No. 1) stands the Preussische Pfandbrief-{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Str. side), Archimedes, Leonardo da Vinci, James Watt, and Ste¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}phenson (on the Voss-Str. side). The extensive block at the opposite{% endraw %}
{% raw %}corner (No. 35) is the Ministry of Public Works, including the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Imperial Office of Railways. At Nos. 4 & 5 in the Voss-Str. is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Reichs-Justizamt ('Imperial Justice Office'), and there are also{% endraw %}
{% raw %}many handsome private residences in this street.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A little to the S. of the W. end of the Voss-Str. lies the Pots¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}damer-Platz (p. 127), adjoined on the E. by the site of the old Pots¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dam Gate and the octagonal Leipziger-Platz (PI. R, 19), which is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adorned with gardens and statues of Count Brandenburg (to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left; d. 1850), the general and statesman, by Hagen, and Field-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Marshal Count Wrangel (to the right; d. 1877), by Keil. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}former was erected in 1862, the latter in 1880. At Nos. 6-10 in this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Platz is the Ministry of Agriculture, Domains, and Forests;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}No. 13, on the N. side, is the Admiralty (Reichs-Marine-Amt);{% endraw %}
{% raw %}No. 14 the offices of the Berlin Tramway Co. (p. 14); No. 15 Herr{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mosse's House, built by Ebe and Benda, with a sandstone frieze by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Max Klein representing the development of the German genius; and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}No. 16 the House of the Imperial Automobile Club, designed by Ihne.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the Leipziger-Platz the busy *Leipziger-Strasse (PI.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}R, 19, 22), about 1 M. in length, crosses the Friedrich-Str. and'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}runs E. to the Spittel-Markt. It is perhaps the chief artery of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traffic in Berlin (numerous tramways to the West End; see p. 126),{% endraw %}

{% raw %}commercial buildings, most of which are in the Renaissance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}style. The visitor is advised to inspect this street in the evening,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}when the shop-windows are brilliantly lighted.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}on the groundfloor of which is the attractive dep6t of the Royal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Porcelain Manufactory (p. 179), which may be visited at any hour.—{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adjacent (Nos. 3-4) are the Herrenhaus and (No. 5) the War Office.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}passage on the E. side), and encloses a spacious fore-court, on either{% endraw %}
{% raw %}side of which are dwellings for the presidents of the two chambers.{% endraw %}
