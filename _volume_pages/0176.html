{% raw %}138 Section 6. BERLIN. Konigs-Platz.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the W. of the Wandel-Halle is the Reading Room, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}panelled walls and ceiling. About 400 newspapers are laid out here.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The frieze of putti is by M. Koch; the wall-paintings, representing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Marienburg, the Wartburg, the Harbour of Hamburg, Arcona,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Frauen-Kirche at Dresden, Speyer Cathedral, and Tangermunde,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are by Ludwig, Kiihl, Prell, Dill, Hertel, and Brucht. — The ad¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}jacent Writing Room, in the N.W. tower, is also finished throughout{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in wood. In the corners are wooden statues of Vulcan, Neptune,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mercury, and Ceres. The wall-paintings (Strassburg, the Wendel-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stein, the Teufels-Schlucht, the Chiemsee, and the Bastei) are by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Schbnleber, Ludwig, Bracht, and Ruupp.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The central space beneath the great glass dome is occupied by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the *Hall op the Diet, 95 ft. long, 69 ft. wide, and 43 ft. high.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The walls are panelled in light oak and adorned with ornamental{% endraw %}
{% raw %}designs, coats-of-arms, and figures. Beneath the glass roof runs a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}circular vaulting, embellished with coats-of-arms. In the middle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the E. side is the seat of the president, beside which is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}secretaries' desk. In front is the tribune, whence members address{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the house, flanked by the places for the ministers and members of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Federal Council. Immediately in front of the tribune are the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seats of the shorthand-writers and beyond these, the table of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}house. The seats for the 397 deputies are arranged amphitheatri-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cally, facing the president, to whose 'right' and 'left' sit the political{% endraw %}
{% raw %}parties grouped under these names. The doors on the N. and S.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}leading to the division lobbies, are embellished with intarsia designs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}representing Ulysses escaping from Polyphemus and Rubezahl{% endraw %}
{% raw %}('Number Nip'). — The hall is surrounded by a broad panelled{% endraw %}
{% raw %}corridor, the beams of which are supported by small half-figures,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}each bearing a gilded letter of the motto 'Erst das Vaterland, dann{% endraw %}
{% raw %}die Partei' ('country before party').{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the W. of the S. end of the Wandel-Halle are the Refreshment{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rooms. The main room has a barrel-vault, embellished by Hupp{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with coats-of-arms and festoons of thistles, amid which are sportive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}putti with the imperial insignia. — The ceiling of the corner-room{% endraw %}
{% raw %}displays the phases of the moon in metal; in the angles of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vaulting are low reliefs of the four elements.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We now descend to the S. Vestibule, with rich Renaissance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}portals in sandstone, by Vogel. Above that to the E. are the arms{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Prussia supported by Wisdom and Strength; above that to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}W. the arms of Bavaria, with Justice and Unity. The stained-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}glass windows are both by Linuemann: to the N. is seen the im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}perial eagle, bearing the arms of the federal states on its wings;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}over the S. portal is Germania. — In front of the columns are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}eight colossal bronze statues of early emperors, viz. Charlemagne,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Breuer, Henry I., by Briitt, Otho I., by Maison, Henry III.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Manzel, Frederick Barbarossa, by Baumbach, Rudolf of Haps-{% endraw %}
