{% raw %}62 Section 1.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}BERLIN.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Arsenal.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E. Wing. On entering from the E. Hall of the Generals, we have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Oriental weapons to our right and the European to our left. Special{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attention should be paid to the beautiful 15th cent, armour and the fine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gala suits of the 16th cent., mostly bequeathed by Prince Charles of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Prussia (d. 1883), and many of them adorned with reliefs. The chief{% endraw %}
{% raw %}specimens are on or near the central pillars and by the windows. No. 60a.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}helmet with the Judgment of Paris and Abduction of Helen; 59a & b. state{% endraw %}
{% raw %}armour of Elector Joachim II., of 1539; 76. part of a suit of armour with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Roman battles; (window-side) 64. shield with the Judgment of Paris (after{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Raphael), 73. with cavalry contest, 74. with engagement under the walls{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of a fortress; 75. armour of Emperor Charles V.; 82. shields with Curtius{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Hercules; 84. field suit of Joachim II., 1560; shields with Horatius{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Codes (87), and the Fall of the Giants (95); 105. armour of the Margrave{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hans of Kilstrin. — In the corner: ornamented cross-bows (125, 133) and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}portable fire-arms (130-132), calendar blades (131).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Wing. Chiefly Prussian arms and uniforms up to the time of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Frederick the Great. In the middle of the side next the court are uni¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}forms and orders of the Emperors William I. and Frederick III., orders{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Bismarck and Moltke, and keys of the French fortresses captured in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1814 and 1870-71. — On the side next the street, near the middle; swords{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(197) of the Brandenburg Electors, and (198) with portraits of Emperors{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Leopold, Rudolph II., and Ferdinand II. — Beyond the centre, 232. sou¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}venirs of Derfflinger; 233. standards of Henning von Treffenfeld with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}symbolic devices. These are followed by uniforms of the 18th century;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on case 278, ensign of a Prussian recruiting officer of the 18th century;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tigerskin and bearskin-cap of Zieten; 302-7. figures of the soldiers of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Frederick the Great. On the adjoining pillars are portraits of the tall{% endraw %}
{% raw %}grenadiers of Frederick William I.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The W. Wing is specially devoted to arms and uniforms of the time{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Wars of Liberation (1813 -1815). At the end of the side next the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}street: 417. figures of Prussian soldiers of the period; 412. orders, hat,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and pistols of Napoleon I., captured at Waterloo; 411. uniforms of Fre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}derick William III.; 423. souvenirs of Blucher. — On both sides of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}central passage are 68 *Lifesize Figures of Prussian Soldiers. Those to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left represent soldiers from the time of the Great Elector down to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1806 and also the royal household troops; to the right, those from 1806{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the present time, ending with the troops in the colonies and the China{% endraw %}
{% raw %}expedition (1900).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the S. side of the Zeughaus - Platz is the Palace of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Crown Prince (PI. R, 23), which is connected with the so-called{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Palace of the Princesses by an arch over the Oberwall-Strasse.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Built in 1663 as a private mansion, it was rebuilt in 1732 for Fre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}derick the Great when Crown Prince, and from 1793 to 1840 was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}occupied by Frederick William III. In 1797 Emperor William I.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was born here.. It owes its present form to the alterations made in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1857 by Struck, who added the second story, and from 1858 to 1888{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it was the winter residence of the Crown Prince Frederick William{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Emp. Frederick III.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The last house on this side is the Residence of the Commandant{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Berlin. — Behind is the Schiukel-Platz, see p. 158.{% endraw %}
