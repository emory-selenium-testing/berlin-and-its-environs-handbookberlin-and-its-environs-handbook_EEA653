{% raw %}70 Section 2. BERLIN. Royal Stables.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(tramways No. 17, 60, 61, 63, 70, 73, 78, 80, and 81; see pp. 16-20) is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Sehloss-Brunnen, a monumental fountain by R. Begas, un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}veiled in 1891. In the centre is Neptune on a rocky throne, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tritons and putti below. The basin, 59 ft. in diameter, contains four{% endraw %}
{% raw %}marine animals, while round the brim are figures of the Rhine, Oder,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Elbe, and Vistula.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the S. side of the Schloss-Platz, between the Spree and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Breite-Str., are the Royal Stables (PI. R, 25, 26), erected by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ihne in the Renaissance style in 1897-1900. The main front is 305 ft.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}long, while the river-front is 578 ft. in length. The building is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adorned with sculptures by Otto Lessing. On the attic are horse-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tamers and figures of ancient warriors, while at each end of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}main front is a fountain inserted in the wall, that to the left with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Prometheus and the Oceanidae, that to the right with Perseus and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Andromeda.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adm. to the stables (entrance, Breite-Str. 37), see p. 39. — The horses{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(350) are kept in two stories of the river-wing, with the carriages above.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The small Historical Museum arranged here contains the sledge on which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Great Elector pursued the Swedes across the Kurische Haft in 1679;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the white charger which Frederick the Great bestrode at the battle of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mollwitz; Sadowa, a favourite horse of William I.; and state carriages and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sledges of the time of the first kings. — The main wing contains state{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carriages, saddle and harness rooms; in the middle the coronation coach,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}built at Strassburg in 1793.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Kurfursten-Briicke (Bridge of the Elector; PI. R, 26){% endraw %}
{% raw %}leads to the E. from the Schloss-Platz to the old town of Berlin.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The bridge, built in 1692-95 after designs by Nering and rebuilt in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the old style in 1895, affords a good view of the river-front of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Royal Palace (p. 65), the Cathedral (p. 63), etc.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The bridge is adorned with a bronze equestrian *Statue of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Great Elector (d. 1688), designed by Schliiter and erected in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1703. This clever and artistic group is one of the few really good{% endraw %}
{% raw %}works of a period when art was generally in a very debased condition.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In spite of the Roman costume, the figure is remarkable for its air{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of majestic repose, which is heightened by contrast with the move¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ments of the four slaves round the pedestal intended to typify the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hostile powers against whom the Elector had waged war. The reliefs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the sides represent (left) the Electorate and the Old Palace, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(right) the Kingdom and the New Palace (original pedestal in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Emp. Frederick Museum, see p. 97).{% endraw %}
