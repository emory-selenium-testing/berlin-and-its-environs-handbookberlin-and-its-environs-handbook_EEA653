{% raw %}A broad flight of steps, 66 ft. in height, intersected by six{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Terraces, ascends from the great fountain to the palace. On the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}highest terrace two elegant fountains project their water in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}form of bells. Frederick the Great's greyhounds are buried at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E. end of this terrace. The king expressed a wish to be buried at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the foot of the statue of Flora here ('Quand je serai la, je serai{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sans souci').{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *Palace of Sanssouci, a building of one story, 320 ft. in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}length and 39 ft. high, erected by Knobelsdorff for Frederick the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Great from the king's own sketches in 1745-47, and that monarch's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}almost constant residence, stands on an eminence above the town.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The terrace on the N. side is enclosed by a semicircular Corinthian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}colonnade. Frederick's rooms are still preserved almost unaltered;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}they are interesting on account of their rococo decorations as well{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as for their historical associations (adm., see p. 189; tickets ob¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tained at the rear of the palace).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The West Wing contains Voltaire's Room, with interesting wood-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carving and a porcelain bust of Voltaire ('immortalis'), and Fred. Wil¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}liam IV.'s Rooms, in which he died in 1861. — In the Central Building{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the oval Dining Room, the S. side of which can be thrown open to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the terrace. The figures of Apollo and Urania are by Fr. G. Adam (p. 193).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The king's famous round table is well known from Menzel's painting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 92). — East Wing. The Audience Room and the adjacent apartments{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are hung with admirable *Paintings by Watteau, Pater, and Lancret, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contain handsome furniture. — The clock in the Concert Saloon, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Frederick was in the habit of winding up, is said to have stopped at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}precise moment of his death (2.20 a.m., 17th Aug., 1786). The wall-paintings{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in this room are by Pesne; handsome ceiling. — Frederick the Great's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Apartment. This room, in which he died, was reconstructed the same{% endraw %}
{% raw %}year by his successor. Graff's portrait of Frederick represents him at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about the age of fifty-six. Marble *Statue by Magnussen (1898), repre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}senting the 'last moments of Frederick the Great'. In the alcove is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}portrait of the king's mother, Sophie Dorothea, by Pesne. — The *Library,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}charmingly decorated in the rococo style in cedar-wood and gilt-bronze,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contains a few antique busts, the best of which is one of Homer. Spe¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cimens of the king's handwriting and a drawing (design of the palace) by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}him. — The Gallery, with paintings by Watteau and his school, leads to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Parole Room, behind the dining-room, where our visit terminates.{% endraw %}
