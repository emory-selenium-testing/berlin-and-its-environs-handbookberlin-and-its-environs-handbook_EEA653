{% raw %}In the grounds between the Library and the Opera House stands{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a marble monument, by Schaper, to the Empress Augusta, unveiled{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1895. The reliefs on the pedestal represent the nursing of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wounded in war and the education of children. — In the background{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the Roman Catholic Church of St. Hedwig (Hedwigs-Kirche ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}PI. R, 22), a simplified imitation of the Pantheon at Rome, erected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Frederick the Great in 1747-73. The dome was sheathed with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}copper in 1886-87 and provided with the lantern and cross demanded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the spirit of the original design. The representation of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adoration of the Magi in the pediment was executed in 1898 by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}N. Geiger. — Close by, Behren-Str. 38-39, is the Dresdner Bank,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}built in 1887 by Heim and enlarged in 1902.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Five *Statues by Rauch embellish the Platz am Opernhaus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}farther E. Between the Opera House and the palace of the crown-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prince is Blucher (d. 1819), a bronze figure, 11 ft. high, with drawn{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sword, resting one foot on a cannon, erected in 1826. To the left and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right are bronze statues of Yorck (d. 1830) and Gneisenau (d. 1831),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}both erected in 1855. In front of the guard-house are marble fig¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ures of Billow (d. 1816) and Scharnhorst (d. 1813), erected in 1822.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The pedestals are adorned with reliefs referring to the wars of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1813-15.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Royal Guard House (Konigswache; PI. R, 23) was con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}structed by Schinkel in the Doric style in 1816-18 in the form of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Roman fortified gate. Adjoining it are three large cannon; the cen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tral one ('La belle Josephine') was brought from Fort Mont Valerien{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at Paris in 1871 and has been rechristened 'Valeria'.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the back of the guard-house is the Ministry of Finance. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the left of this, and farther back, is the Singing Academy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PI. R, 23), erected in 1825 and renowned for the excellent acoustic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}properties of its concert-hall. The academy was founded by Fasch{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1791, and afterwards came under the management of Zelter{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(d. 1832). Concerts, see p. 29.{% endraw %}
