{% raw %}178 Section 11. BERLIN. Zoolog. Garden.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Birds' House are in the Japanese style. The new Aviary is in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Moorish style, as is also the Antelope House, which has a painting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on majolica, by P. Meyerheim. The group of centaurs in front of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}latter is by R. Begas. The Elephant House is in the form of an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Indian pagoda; while an ancient Egyptian style has been adopted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for the Ostrich House, and Russian, Siamese, and Alaskan buildings{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have served as models for the Buffalo Houses. The View Tower{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(ascent 10 pf.) may also be mentioned. — Exhibition Hall, see p. 181.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The collection of animals embraces upwards of 1300 different{% endraw %}
{% raw %}kinds, several here shown for the first time in any zoological garden.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Many interesting specimens have recently been brought from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}German colonies. Among the beasts of prey mention may be made{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the N. varieties of the tiger, leopard, and puma, which remain{% endraw %}
{% raw %}here in the open air even in winter. The completed wing of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}great aviary contains 400 separate cages for foreign birds; among{% endraw %}
{% raw %}130 varieties of parrots, 20 are green Amazon-parrots. Nearly every{% endraw %}
{% raw %}known variety of cranes and storks is represented. Many rarities{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are to be found also among the deer (50 varieties), horses, wild{% endraw %}
{% raw %}goats, wild sheep, and wild cattle. — The animals are usually fed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}between 6 and 7 p.m. from June to September. Wed. is a fast-day{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for the carnivora. Comp. the lists exhibited at various points in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the garden.{% endraw %}
