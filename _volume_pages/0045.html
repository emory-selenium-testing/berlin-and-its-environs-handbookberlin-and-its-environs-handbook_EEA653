{% raw %}k. Post, Telegraph, and Telephone Offices.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The addresses of the nearest post-offices, etc., are given on the letter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}boxes and advertising-pillars. Inquiry Office for all postal matters, Konig-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Str. 61. Official postal guide ('Postbuch') for Berlin and neighbourhood,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}60 pf.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Post Offices. The Central Post Office is at Spandauer-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Str. 19-24 (PL R, 26; p. 151). The town is divided into nine postal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}districts, designed by letters, C, N, NO, O, etc. (comp. the list of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}streets, p. 229). Letters for Berlin should contain the district initial{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in their address and, if possible, also the number of the post-office{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by which they are delivered. There are 113 post-offices and branch-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}offices, almost all of which have telegraph, pneumatic post, and tele¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}phone offices. No parcels are received by the branch-offices. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}most important post-offices are the following: CI, Hof-Postamt,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Spandauer-Str. 19-24; C2, Heiligegeist-Str. 24-33; letters to be call¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ed for ('Postlagernd' or 'Poste Restante') and Money Orders, when{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not addressed to any particular post-office, are distributed here. Then,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}JV3,Paket-Postamt (Parcel Post Office), Oranienburger-Str. 70-72;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}NW7, Dorotheen-Str. 23-24; W8, Tauben-Str. 8-9; SW12, Zim-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mer-Str. 27; SW 19, Beuth-Str. 18-21; C25, Am Kiinigsgraben 17 ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the offices at the six chief railway stations (Anhalt, Potsdam,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lehrte, Stettin, Silesia, and Gorlitz). The post-offices are open from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}7 (in winter from N) a.m. to 8 p.m. (branch-offices from 8 a.m. to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}7 p.m.); for parcels till 7 p.m.; on Sundays and holidays the dis¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trict offices are open from 7 (in winter 8) to 9 a.m., and 12 to 1 p.m.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the branch-offices remaining closed. The postage for letters within{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the city and its neighbourhood is 5 pf., for post-cards also 5 pf.;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for other parts of Germany 10 and 5 pf.; for abroad 20 and 10 pf.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Parcels from abroad are examined by custom-house officers at Ritter-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Str. 7, Kloster-Str. 76, Kothener-Str. 28, or Schiffbauerdamm 22,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}according to the district of the city in which the addressee lives.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A system of Pneumatic Tubes (Rohrpost) also exists, for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the rapid transmission of letters and post-cards from one part of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Berlin to another (including Charlottenburg). Letters (30 pf.), which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}must not exceed a certain size and weight, or post-cards (25 pf., reply{% endraw %}
{% raw %}post-cards 50 pf.) intended for transmission by this service must{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be marked 'Rohrpost' in the upper left-hand corner of the address.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Post-offices with pneumatic service (open from 7 or 8 a.m. till 10p.m.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}are distinguished by a red lamp (comp. above). Letters and post¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cards sent by this service reach their destination in 1-2 hrs., but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}only when posted at a pneumatic post office.{% endraw %}
