{% raw %}Preface.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The present Handbook for Berlin, which corresponds with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fifteenth German edition, incorporates, in a much expanded and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carefully revised form, information heretofore included in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Handbook for Northern Germany. Its chief object, like that of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Editor's other guides, is to render the traveller as nearly as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}possible independent of the services of guides and others and to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enable him to employ his time, his money, and his energy to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}best advantage.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Though Berlin does not compete in antiquity or historical in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}terest with the other great European capitals, its position as the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}metropolis of the German empire and its wealth of art-treasures,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}both ancient and modern, invest it with high importance in addition{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to its special and characteristic interest as the greatest purely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}modern city in Europe. While devoting particular attention to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the description of the imposing imperial and national edifices and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the great public collections, the Editor has not forgotten this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}last-mentioned phase, and has endeavoured to include as com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prehensive a selection as possible of other objects of general interest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for the cultivated traveller. No one is better aware than the Editor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}himself of the difficulty of securing absolute accuracy in a guide¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}book; and he will therefore gratefully welcome the continuance of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}those valuable corrections and suggestions with which travellers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have long been in the habit of favouring him.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The utmost care has been bestowed upon the Maps and Plans.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The subdivision of the large Plan of Berlin (at the end of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}book) into three sections of different colours will be found materially{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to facilitate reference, as it obviates the necessity of unfolding a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}large sheet of paper at each consultation. The area shown on this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}plan coincides with that given in reduced scale on the Tramway{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Plan at p. 14, so that reference from one plan to the other presents{% endraw %}
{% raw %}no difficulty. The numbersappearingontheTramway Plan correspond{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the official numbers of the tramway-lines. A plan of the inner{% endraw %}
{% raw %}town on a larger scale has been added to the present edition.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The list of Hotels and Restaurants given in the Handbook{% endraw %}
{% raw %}comprises the most important establishments and many of humbler{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pretensions. Those which the Editor has reason to believe especially{% endraw %}
{% raw %}worthy of commendation in proportion to their charges are denoted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by asterisks. The Editor has distributed these marks of commend¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ation (the value of which is relative only) as fully and impartially{% endraw %}
