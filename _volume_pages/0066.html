{% raw %}BERLIN.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}History.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Friedrichs - Werder (on the left bank of the Spree, between the Arse¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nal and the Spittel-Markt), and Neu-Kollu (the Wall-Strasse and its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}neighbourhood). These form the heart of the city and were origin¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ally enclosed by fortifications. Round them in the 17th and 18th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cent, rose an inner zone of new quarters, bounded until 1868 by a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}customs-wall 9 M. in length (almost corresponding with the present{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Stadtring' of the electric tramway, p. 14): the Dorotheenstadt{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(stretching from the Spree to the Behren-Strasse), Friedrichstadt{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(to the S. of the Behren-Strasse), Luisenstadt (to the E. of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Linden-Strasse), etc. To these a large number of suburbs now in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}corporated in the town were added during the 19th century.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The government of the city is shared by the Royal Police De¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}partment, the Civic Magistracy, and the Town Council, under a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Chief Burgomaster and a Burgomaster. The magistracy consists of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}34members, of whom 18 are salaried. There are 144 town councillors.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The municipal revenues for 1908 were estimated at 8 millions ster¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ling. — Berlin is divided into six electoral districts for the Reichstag{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Imperial Diet), and four for the Landtag (Prussian Chamber of De¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}puties) , in the latter of which it is represented by nine deputies.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}History. Berlin first appears in history in the early part of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 13th cent., when it was already a double town of some import¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ance (Berlin and Kolln). In all probability the original Wendish{% endraw %}
{% raw %}settlements had been taken possession of by German colonists at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the end of the preceding century. The name also is doubtless of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Wendish origin, and its connection with the bear (Bar) which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}appears in the city arms is merely an etymological fancy of later{% endraw %}
{% raw %}date. The name of Kolln is mentioned for the first time in a doc¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ument of 1237, that of Berlin in 1244, after which the town soon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}began to be regarded as one of the most considerable in the Mark,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ranking with Brandenburg (p. 205), the residence of the Margraves.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The two towns were united in 1307, and by their prudent policy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}during the troublous times of the 14th cent, raised themselves to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a position of great importance. Berlin-Kolln even became head of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a confederation of towns of the Mark, which it represented in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dealings with the Hanseatic League. Such a position, however,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}almost amounting to the independence of a free imperial town, could{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not be maintained against the might of the Hohenzollern family,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who became masters of the Mark in 1415. In consequence of its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}unsuccessful opposition to Frederick '■with the Iron Tooth' (1440-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}70), the second Elector, the town was deprived of its privileges{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(1442, 1448), and a fortified castle was erected to keep it in check.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Electm- John Cicero (1486-99) made Berlin-Kolln his permanent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}abode, and since that period the fortunes of the town have been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interwoven with those of the Hohenzollern family and their other{% endraw %}
