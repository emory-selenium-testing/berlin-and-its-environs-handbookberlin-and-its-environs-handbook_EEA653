{% raw %}The *R,oyal Palace (Konigliches Schloss; PI. R, 26) is in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}form of a rectangle 630 ft. in length and 381 ft. in depth, enclosing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two large courts, which are entered by five portals, each bearing a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}number. The facade rises in four stories to the height of 98 ft., while{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the dome above it is 232ft. high. The original building was a castle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected by Elector Frederick II. on the Spree in 1443-51. Since{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1538 Joachim II. converted it into a palace after the designs of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Kaspar Theyss, with a tilting-yard on the S. side, the whole forming{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a handsome example of the German Renaissance, highly praised by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contemporary critics. Elector John George finished the enclosure{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the E. court in 1580-95; from his time date the water-front on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Spree with its corner-turrets (PI. 16) and the 'Schloss-Apotheke'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(chemist's shop), protruding towards the N. and recently shortened.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A second court was added (to the W.) and the N.E. corner completed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Under the Great Elector, who at first directed his energies to laying{% endraw %}
{% raw %}out the Lustgarten, Nering erected the round-arched gallery on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Spree (PI. 15) in 1685. The Elector's successor, Frederick I., the first{% endraw %}
{% raw %}King of Prussia, determined to replace the irregular pile of build¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ings that had now arisen by a uniform structure of massive and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}imposing proportions, and confided the execution of this task to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Andreas Schliiter, who began his work in 1698. The gigantic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scheme of alteration thus projected has, however, never been fully{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carried out, the part of the building on the Spree and the transverse{% endraw %}
{% raw %}building still retaining their original form. In 1706 Schltlter retired{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the direction of the work, as the rebuilding of the so-called{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Miinzturm', which was to be some 300 ft. high (at the N.W. corner,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where the column with the eagle now stands), had failed. He was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}succeeded by Johunn Friedrich Eosander, surnamed von Goethe,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a native of Sweden, to whom is due the architecture of the largest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}court and of the W- facade. In 1716 the process of alteration was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brought by Bbhme to a conclusion for the nonce, and during the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reigns of Frederick II. and Frederick William II. comparatively{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trifling additions only were made. In 1825-26 Schinkel restored{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the apartments of Frederick II. for the Crown Prince. The spa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cious chapel in the W. wing, with its dome, was constructed in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reign of Frederick William IV. by Staler and Schadow (1845-52),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and greatly enhances the effect of the exterior. A new period of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}building activity began under Emp. William II., who made the pal¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ace once more the actual residence of the reigning sovereign. Great{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gates of wrought iron were placed in the five outer portals; a ter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}race with a landing-stage was constructed on the river-side; and,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}finally, the W. wing, with the White Saloon (p. 68) and the neigh¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bouring apartments, has recently undergone a thorough reconstruc¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion from the designs of Pine.{% endraw %}
