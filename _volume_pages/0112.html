{% raw %}The following are the more important groups and figures, beginning{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at the steps and proceeding from left to right. On the Front: Dionysos.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the Right Side: Various deities, including Cybele mounted on a lion,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in combat with a monster in the form of a bull; Selene on horseback;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Helios in floating drapery in his chariot; Aurora riding through the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}heavens; a lion-headed giant being strangled by a youth; Phrebe and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}her opponents; Asteria supported by a dog. On the Back: Bearded giant{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in combat with the triple-headed Hecate; Artemis and the youthful Otos;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tityos hard pressed by the torch of Leto; magnificent torso of Apollo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the background. Further towards the end are the main groups: Zeus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}destroying three opponents with thunderbolts, and Athena grasping Al-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}kyoneus (son of Gaea, the earth-goddess) by the hair (the Alkyoneus is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interesting on account of the resemblance to the Laocoon). On the Left{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Side: Castor defending himself against Idas; Nyx hurling a vessel en¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}circled by a serpent; a goddess, supposed to be Medusa, supported by a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lion. On the Front and on the adjacent side-wall of the staircase are marine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}deities, including the fantastic form of Triton, and the mighty Oceanus.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the platform at the top of the flight of steps stood the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sacrificial altar, which was surrounded by a low colonnade, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}elegant Ionic columns. The inner side of the rear-wall was decor¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ated with the Telephos Frieze, 5 ft. in height, depicting the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}history of Telephos, the mythical founder of Pergamon. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}numerous and in part uninterpreted fragments are exhibited on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the entrance-wall of the gangway (beginning beyond the corner to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the right of the entranee; inscriptions below the reliefs). — On the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other walls are other sculptures discovered at Pergamon, frag¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ments of architecture, and inscriptions. A series of large Female{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Figures is noteworthy, and still more so a beautiful ^Female Head{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(opposite the E. wall of the altar, near the left corner), recalling{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Venus of Milo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Inner Court (closed), within the reconstructed large altar,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contains architectural fragments illustrating the more important{% endraw %}
{% raw %}buildings. By the entrance-wall and rear-wall are fragments from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pergamon (by the entrance-wall a fragment of the sacrificial altar{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also); to the left, fragments from Magnesia; and to the right those{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from Priene. Here too, opposite the entrance, is a large marble{% endraw %}
{% raw %}copy from Pergamon of the statue of Athena Parthenos by Phidias.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— Outside the entrance to the Museum is a semicircular marble{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bench from Pergamon.{% endraw %}
