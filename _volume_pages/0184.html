{% raw %}Passing through a vestibule, we first enter the Small Synagogue,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}used for the daily services, beyond which is the Chief Synagogue,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}130 ft. long (not including the apse), 79 ft. broad, and 78% ft. high,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with 3000 seats. The iron vaulted roof is supported by slender{% endraw %}
{% raw %}iron pillars. During the evening-service (Fridays at dusk) the 'dim{% endraw %}
{% raw %}religious light' from the stained glass and the cupolas produces a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}remarkably fine effect. The places for men are on the groundfloor,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}those for women in the galleries.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Near the E. end of the Oranienburger-Str. is the Monbijou-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Platz, embellished with a marble bust of Chamisso (p. 171), by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mosen. Tramways see p. 166 (Hackescher Markt). In this Platz is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the entrance to the royal —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Chateau of Monbijou (PI. R, 23). The nucleus of the edifice{% endraw %}
{% raw %}consists of a villa erected by Eosauder von Goethe in 1706 for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Countess Wartenberg; the two detached buildings facing the Mon-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bijou-Platz were added by Unger in 1789-90. In the Monbijou{% endraw %}
{% raw %}garden is the tasteful little English Church (St. George's),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected in 1884-85 by Raschdorff (services, see p. 36).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Chateau has since 1877 contained the *Hohenzollern{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Museum, a collection of personal reminiscences of the Prussian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rulers from the time of the Great Elector down to the present day.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It includes a large number of objects of genuine artistic interest,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and affords an excellent survey of the progress of industrial art in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the last two centuries. Director, Prof. Seidel. Adm., see p. 38.{% endraw %}
