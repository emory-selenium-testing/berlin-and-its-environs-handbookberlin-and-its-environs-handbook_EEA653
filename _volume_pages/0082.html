{% raw %}BERLIN.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Arsenal.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}best buildings in Berlin, begun by Nering in 1694 and carried to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}completion by Grilnberg, Schliiter (1698-99), and De Bodt (1706).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It is a square structure, each side of which is 295 ft. in length,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enclosing a quadrangle 125 ft. square. The exterior is richly adorned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with sculptures by Schliiter. The bronze bust of Frederick I. above{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the main portal and the allegorical figures to the right and left are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Hulot. In 1877-80 the interior underwent a thorough alteration{% endraw %}
{% raw %}under the superintendence of Hitzig (d. 1881), and it was re-opened{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as a Military Museum and Hall of Fame of the Prussian Army.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The artistic decoration was completed in 1891. The director of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}collections is Dr. von Ubisch (adm., see p. 36; official handbook{% endraw %}
{% raw %}50 pf.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The rooms of the Ground Floor, unfortunately somewhat poorly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lighted, are separated by handsome iron railings and adorned with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mural paintings in grisaille (siege operations) by Burger. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rooms to the right (E.) of the vestibule contain the Museum of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Artillery, those to the left (W.) are devoted to the Museum of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Military Engineering.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The collection of artillery is nearly complete from the end of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}14th century onwards, though the number of fine artistic specimens{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is comparatively small. The following objects are specially worthy of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attention: (to the left) between pillars 1 and 2 Chinese guns captured in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1900, among them No. 603. Chinese gun, cast in 1689 by Verbiest, a Jesuit{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(comp. j. 195); (to the right) old flint-lock muskets and orgues; No. 66.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The 'Wild Man', the longest gun (19 ft.) in the collection; No. 130 (in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}corner), so-called golden cannon of 1641; adjacent, 17th cent, cannon made{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of leather; No. 157. richly chased 48-pounder, made at Ltibeck in 1669 for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Holland and discovered at Paris in 1814; Nos. 168, 169, 185, and 186 (be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tween the pillars), cannon of the time of the Great Elector, the last{% endraw %}
{% raw %}covered with ornamentation; farther on, opposite a balloon-gun of 1870-71,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a cannon dedicated to the Elector Albert Achilles, one of eleven cast by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Jacobi in 1708 by command of King Frederick I.; No. 361 (near the end{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the room), orgue used by Schill's volunteers, who made in 1809 a brave{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but unsuccessful attempt to free Prussia from the French yoke.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Museum of Military Engineering (left side) contains models of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}objects connected with pioneering and artillery. Here also are models{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of old French fortresses (including Sedan and Paris), brought from Paris{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1814; model-plans of the battles of Diippel (1864), Koniggratz (1866),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and St. Privat (1870); and uniforms (to the left Austrian, French, British,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}etc., to the right Russian). At the end a magnificent Turkish tent, cap¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tured in 1683 before Vienna.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Opposite the vestibule is the entrance to the glass-roofed Court,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the centre of which is occupied by a colossal marble figure of Bo-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}russia, by R. Begas. The *Heads of Dying Warriors on the key¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stones of the windows (widely known as the Masks of Schliiter) are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}very striking. The Chinese standards above were captured in 1900.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}First Floor. From the back of the court two flights of steps,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adorned with sculptures by R. Begas, ascend to the Hall op Fame,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which occupies the N. wing of the Upper Story and consists of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}three sections — the 'Herrscherhalle' in the middle and the two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Feldherrnhallen' at the sides.{% endraw %}
