{% raw %}64 Section 2.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}BERLIN.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cathedral.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Vestibule on the Lustgarten side is two stories high and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}262 ft. long. The chief entrance in the centre is high and vaulted;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}over the arch are two bronze angels holding a shield. The piers on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}both sides of the entrance are adorned with bronze reliefs (to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right, Luther translating the Bible, by Goetz; to the left, Luther at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Worms, by Janensch); above them are bronze statues of SS. Luke{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and John (right) and SS. Matthew and Mark (left). On the impost{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the right and left are bronze figures of Mercy and Truth, modelled{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Widemann. On the attic are two head-pieces ending in crowns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and a centre-piece containing a figure of Christ by Schaper in cop¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}per, 17 ft. in height. The domed towers at the corners, each 262 ft.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}high, contain the bells. On the entablature are ten Apostles by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Manzel, Bruit, Buumbuch, Herter, Calandrelli, and Pfann-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}schmidt. — The Central Dome is octagonal in shape and measures{% endraw %}
{% raw %}125 ft. in external and 102 ft. in internal diameter. The richly orna¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mented drum is surmounted by eight figures of angelic musicians,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}17 ft. in height, by Schott. — The side towards the Spree, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two vestibules adjoining the apse, ends in two smaller towers with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cupolas, beneath which, in niches, are two carved angels taken from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the old cathedral. Above, next the apse, are figures of Moses, by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Janensch, and John the Baptist, by Vogel.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior (adm., see p. 36) of the cathedral is tripartite. The Church{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Proper, to which the main entrance admits, is surmounted by the dome.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The vaulting is adorned with mosaic representations of the Eight Beati¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tudes, after A. von Werner; the four spandrels with decorative carvings{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the history of the Apostles, by 0, Lessiny. Eight colossal statues{% endraw %}
{% raw %}surround the base of the drum: Luther and Melanchthon (by Pfannschmidt),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Zwingli (by Janensch), Calvin (by Calandrelli), Frederick the Wise (by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}K. Begas), Joachim II. (by Magnussen), Philip the Magnanimous (by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Schott), and Albert of Prussia (by Baumbach). The body of the church{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is in the form of an irregular octagon, and has seats for 1960 worshippers.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the semicircular niches on the shorter sides are the pulpit and galleries.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the longer sides are galleries for the court (W.), the ministers (S.),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the organ and choir (N.). On the E. is the semicircular apse, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dark-red marble columns, sumptuously fitted up with sculpture and gild¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing, and lighted by beautiful stained-glass windows. — To the S. lies the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Wedding and Baptismal Church (not accessible), 59 ft. long and 30 ft.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wide; it is roofed with barrel-vaulting and is adorned with sculptures{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the exterior by 0. Lessing. — To the N. is the Memorial Church,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}115 ft. in length and breadth. The pentagonal central space is surrounded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by small chapels, to contain various monuments. Among these are a mo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nument of Bismarck by R. Begas, the bronze monument of Elector John{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cicero (d. 1499), by Peter Vischer and his son, finished in 1530, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two representations of the deceased (see p. 151); the zinc state coffins of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Great Elector (d. 1688) and his consort Dorothea (d. 1689), of King{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Frederick I. (d. 1713) and his consort Sophia Charlotte (d. 1705), the last{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two from designs by Schliiter; and the marble sarcophagus of Emp. Fre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}derick III., by R. Begas (comp. p. 193). — A staircase on the E. side{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(with a Descent from the Cross by M. Lock) leads from the Memorial{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Church down to the Hohenzollern Burial Vault (not accessible), ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tending beneath the whole cathedral andcontaining) 87 coffins of members{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the reigning family.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}ing of the palace (see p. 65) the Kaiser-Willielm-Briicke (1886-89){% endraw %}
