{% raw %}BERLIN.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}6. Konigs-Platz. Ausstellungs-Park. Sieges-Allee.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Platz vor dem Brandenburger Tor (PI. R, 20, 19) was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}transformed in 1903 after Pine's designs. It is bounded by semi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}circular marble walls and balustrades, in the middle of which are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}marble statues, on the right, of Emperor Frederick III, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the busts of Helmholtz, the physicist, and General Blumenthal,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Briitt; on the left, that of Empress Victoria, with busts of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Zeller, the philosopher, and W. von Hofmann, the chemist, by Gerth.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beyond these, on each side of the Charlottenburg road, are foun¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tains. — Tiergarten, see p. 175; Kbniggratzer-Strasse, p. 127.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Friedens-Allee leads to the right to the *Konigs-Platz{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL R, 20), which with its environs forms one of the most imposing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}parts of the city. Originally laid out as a drill-ground by Frederick{% endraw %}
{% raw %}William I., it received its present name in 1864.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *Column of Victory (Sieges-Sdule; PI. R, 20), 200 ft.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in height, designed by Struck, and inaugurated on 2nd Sept.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1873, rises in the centre of the Platz, on a circular terrace ap¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}proached by eight steps of granite. This massive column, built of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dark red granite, sandstone, and bronze, is perhaps the most im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}posing of its kind in existence. The square pedestal, 22 ft. in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}height, is adorned with reliefs in bronze: on the E. side is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Danish War of 1864, by A. Culundrelli; on the N. the Battle of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Kbniggriitz, 1866, by M. Schultz; on the W. the Battle of Sedan,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1870, and the Entry into Paris, 1871, by K. Keil; on the S. the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Return of the troops to Berlin, 1871, by A. Wolff. The base of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the column is surrounded by an open colonnade, and embellished{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with Venetian mosaics designed by Anton von Werner, illustrating{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the war of 1870 and the restoration of the German empire. Above,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the flutings of the column, are placed three rows of captured{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Danish, Austrian, and French cannon (60 in all). The capital,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}formed of eagles, is crowned with a gilded figure of Victory (by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Drake), holding a laurel-wreath in the right hand and an ensign{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the iron cross in the left. The proportions of the monument{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are so calculated that this surmounting figure, 48 ft. in height, bulks{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as the principal feature. Fine view from the platform of the capital,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}151 ft. high (adm., see p. 37).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Konigs-Platz is bounded on the E. by the *B,eichstags-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gebaude (Hall of the Imperial Diet; PI. R, 20), built in 1884-94{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the designs of Paul Wallot. The building, in the florid{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italian Renaissance style, which cost 22,000,000 marks (1,100,000Z.),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is 430 ft. in length, 290 ft. in breadth, 88 ft. in height to the main{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cornice, and 225 ft. to the imperial crown. The external material{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is Silesian sandstone. Rising above a square central structure is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}huge glass dome, girt with highly,gilded copper bands and bearing{% endraw %}
