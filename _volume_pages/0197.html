{% raw %}To the S. of the Bau-Akademie, beyond the Werder-Str., whence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Schleusen-Briicke leads to the E. to the Schloss-Platz (p. 69), lies{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Unterwasser-Str., which contains the (First) Gerson Bazaar{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and (Nos. 2-4) the Royal Mint (PLR, 25; no admission). The sand¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stone frieze of the latter, executed by Schadow in 1798 from designs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Gilly, represents the processes of obtaining and treating the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}metals, and was enlarged in 1871 by Siemering and Hagen.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Werder'sche Markt, to the W. of the Bau-Akademie, con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tains numerous shops, including (Nos. 5-0) the (Secotul) Gerson{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bazaar. On the N. side is the Friedrichs -Werder Church{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL R, 22), a brick structure erected by Schinkel in 1824-30. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exterior, in modified Gothic, is not happy, but the vaulted interior{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is more pleasing. (Sacristan, Oberwall-Str. 21.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Jager-Strasse, to the S. of the Werder'sche Markt, rises{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(No. 34) the Deutsche Reichsbank, or Bank of the German{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Empire (PL R, 22, 25), a noble brick and sandstone edifice, built{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Hitzig in 1869-76. The bank, which has been enlarged several{% endraw %}
{% raw %}times, now occupies an entire block; the office for securities in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hausvogtei-Platz (S.W.; see below) was built in 1K94 from designs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Emmerich and Hasak. — At the corner of the Jager-Str. and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Oberwall-Str. is the large Central Telegraph and Telephone Office{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PLR, 22; p. 26).{% endraw %}

{% raw %}vogtei', an old house of detention which in its time held many poli¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tical prisoners aud has since been replaced by the Reichsbank (see{% endraw %}
{% raw %}above). The remaining three sides of the 'Platz', which is a centre{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the dress-making trade, are occupied by large business-houses.{% endraw %}
