{% raw %}BERLIN. Unter den Linden.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Unter den Linden, No. 1 (S. side), at the corner of the Pariser-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Platz, is the Hotel Adlon (p. 2), erected by Gause & Leibnitz in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1905-7 on the site of a palace built by Schinkel. On the right,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beyond it, diverges the Wilhelm-Strasse (p. 122), continued on the N.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the Neue Wilhelm-Strasse (p. 161). No. 3, on the right, contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the offices of the Berliner Lokal-Anzeiger, where the latest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}telegrams and original illustrations of events of the day are ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hibited. No. 4, with a sculptured frieze by Eberlein, is the office of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Minister of Religion and Education; Nos. 5 & 6 form the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hotel Bristol (p. 3), by Gause; No. 7 is the palace of the Russian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Embassy, erected by Knoblauch in 1840-41. On the N. side No. 75{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is Schulte's Art Shop (p. 34), built in 1904-5 by Messel; No. 73 is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}occupied by the Minister of the Interior; and Nos. 57-58 are the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}premises of the International Railway Sleeping Car Co. (p. 2),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Berndt (1908).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At No. 68a, on the N. side, is the *Aquarium (PI. R, 20, 23;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entrance in the Sehadow-Str.), founded by Prof. Brehm, and opened{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1869. Director, Dr. Hermes (adm., see p. 36; catalogue 50 pf.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The collection is exhibited in a grotto-like corridor about 300 yds.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in length, which occupies two floors. We first enter the Reptile House,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which contains gigantic lizards and snakes of all kinds, some of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}poisonous varieties attaining a length of 12 ft. The Geological Grotto,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which comes next, contains birds (gulls, cockatoos). This is followed by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the large Bird House, among the inmates of which the weaver-birds{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are specially worthy of notice. Here also are the cages of the anthropoid{% endraw %}
{% raw %}apes, and, to the left and right of the entrance, tanks containing sala¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}manders , crocodiles, and tortoises. After these comes the Aquarium{% endraw %}
{% raw %}proper, with an excellent collection of fresh and salt water fish. We{% endraw %}
{% raw %}finally descend, with breeding-tanks for salmon and trout on either side,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the lower rooms, containing other curiosities of the deep and a refresh¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment-bar.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Farther along the Linden, on the S. side (Nos. 17 & 18), is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Hotel Westminster (p. 3), adjoined to the S. in the Behren-Str.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(passage) by the Metropol-Theater (p. 28). Near the corner of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Friedrich-Str. is the entrance to the Passage or Kaiser-Q-alerie{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PI. R, 23, 22), built in 1869-73 by Kyllmann & Heyden, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}leads to the corner of the Friedrich-Strasse and Behren-Strasse{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 120). The arcade (400 ft. long, 24 ft. broad, and 40 ft. high){% endraw %}
{% raw %}contains a cafe, shops, the Kaiser-Panorama (stereoscopic views;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}open 9 a.m. to 10 p.m., 20 pf.), and the Passage Panopticum (p. 38).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the corner of the Friedrich-Strasse (comp. p. 120) are Kranz-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ler's confectioner's shop, and (No. 25) the Cafe Bauer (No. 26: p. 11).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tramways from the Gendarmen-Markt (S. E.) see p. 121; from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}corner of the Charlotten-Str. and Dorotheen-Str. (N. B.) see p. 145.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beyond the Friedrich-Strasse, on the S. side (No. 35), is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}building of the Diskonto-Gesellschaft, by Ende & Bockmann. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the N. side (No. 39) stands the Grand Hotel de Rome (p. 3).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Close by, Charlotten-Str. 43, is the building of the Society of Ger¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}man Engineers, by Reiiner & Korte.{% endraw %}
