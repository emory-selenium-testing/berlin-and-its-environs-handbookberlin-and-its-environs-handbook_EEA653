{% raw %}Berlin (110-160 ft. above the sea-level; 13° 23' 54" E. long.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}52° 30' 17" N. lat.), the capital of Prussia, the residence of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}German Emperor, and the seat of the imperial government as well{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as of the highest Prussian authorities, contains upwards of 3,000,000{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inhab.f, and thus occupies the third place among the cities of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Europe. Lying in a sandy plain about halfway between the S.W. and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}N.E. extremities of the Empire (465 M. from Mulhausen in Alsace, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}405 M. from Memel), equidistant from the mid-German mountains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the sea, and connected with N.E. Germany and Poland by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}navigable Spree, it is at the same time an important centre of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}railway-system of Germany, one of the foremost seats of commerce{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the country, and perhaps the greatest manufacturing town in con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tinental Europe. The staple commodities of its trade are grain,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spirits, and wool; the principal branches of its industry are iron-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}founding, the construction of machinery, locomotives, and railway-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carriages, and the manufacture of arms, chemicals and textiles, fur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}niture, china, carpets, linoleum, linen, household and fancy goods,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and articles of clothing, the cheaper qualities of which last find{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their way to all parts of the world. The money-market of Berlin{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is also of great importance, and the city has of late taken a leading{% endraw %}
{% raw %}place in the utilization of electric power and the improvement of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lighting facilities.{% endraw %}
