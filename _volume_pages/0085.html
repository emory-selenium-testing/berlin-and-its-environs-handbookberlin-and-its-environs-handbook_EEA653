{% raw %}2. Cathedral. Royal Palace. National Monument{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Emperor William I.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In a straight line with the E. prolongation of the Linden, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spanning the Spree, is the *Schloss-Brueke (Palace Bridge; PI.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}R, 26), 106 ft. in width, constructed in 1822-24 from designs by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Schinkel. It was adorned by Frederick William IV. with eight{% endraw %}
{% raw %}groups in marble, over lifesize, illustrative of the life of a warrior.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the S.: 1. Victory teaches the boy the history of the heroes, by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E. Wolff; 2. Athena instructs the youth in the use of weapons, by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Schievelbein; 3. Athena presents the combatant with arms, by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Moller; 4. Victory crowns the conqueror, by Drake. On the N.:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}5. Victory raises the wounded warrior, by Wichmann; 6. Athena{% endraw %}
{% raw %}protecting and aiding a combatant, by 1>1 User; 7. Athena inciting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}him to a new contest, by A. Wolff; 8. Iris conducts the victorious{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fallen warrior to Olympus, by Wredow.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beyond the bridge extends the Lustgarten (PI. R, 26), an open{% endraw %}
{% raw %}space planted with trees, 247 yds. in length and 220 yds. in width,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}originally a garden belonging to the palace, and afterwards con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}verted into a drill-ground by Frederick William I. In the centre,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on a pedestal of granite 20 ft. in height, rises the equestrian Statue{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Frederick "William TXE., by A. Wolff, 19 ft. in height, un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}veiled in 1871. The pedestal is adorned with allegorical figures of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Clio (in front), Borussia with the Rhine and Memel on the right,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Science with Industry and Art on the left, between them Legisla¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion, and at the back Religion with the olive-branch, a reference to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Union of the Evangelical Confessions (1S17). Military music at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}noon (p. 31). — Beyond the statue, in front of the steps of the Old{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Museum, is a huge Granite Basin, 22 ft. in diameter and 75 tons{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in weight, hewn out of a solid erratic block of ten times the weight.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *Cathedral (Dom; open on week-days 10-6) domin¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ates not only the Lustgarten and its environs, but with its lofty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dome forms the distinguishing feature of any general view of Ber¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lin. It occupies the site of another cathedral built in 1747-50, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}poverty of whose appearance even Schinkel (1816-17) was unable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to remedy, and of the beginnings of a royal vault ('Campo Santo'),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dating from the time of Frederick William IV. (1845-48). The new{% endraw %}
{% raw %}building was erected in 1894-1905 in the style of the developed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italian Renaissance by Julius RascMorff and his son, Otto Rasch-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dorff, at an expense of 10'/2 million marks (525,000 I.). Its dimen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sions are: length 344 ft., breadth 246 ft., height to the main cornice{% endraw %}
{% raw %}102 ft., to the foot of the lantern 246 ft., aud to the top of the cross{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the dome 374 ft. The material is Silesian sandstone, with granite{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for the lower courses of masonry, while the cupolas, lantern, aud{% endraw %}
{% raw %}roofing are of copper.{% endraw %}
