{% raw %}100 Section 3. BERLIN. Emp. Frederick{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The door in the end-wall leads into —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rooms 11 and 12, in which the *Facade of the Palace op{% endraw %}
{% raw %}M'shatta has been erected, a gift of Sultan Abdul Hamid to Em¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}peror William II. M'shatta ('winter-camp') lies about 30 M. to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E. of the influx of the Jordan into the Dead Sea. The palace was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}originally a desert-palace of Ghassanide Bedouins and dates from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 4-6th cent. A.D. The smooth upper wall and part of the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}side of the chief portal are wanting (comp. the plans and models{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exhibited in this room). The part of the facade which has been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brought to Berlin is completely covered with beautiful ornamentation{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in pierced work: from vases, beside which stand animals or fabulons{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beings, spring vines with birds in the branches. The larger left{% endraw %}
{% raw %}half shows an affinity with antique and Byzantine forms, while the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right half, on which are no animals, recalls the Persian art of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sassanide period. — Similar ornamentation appears in the casts{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the portals of ecclesiastical buildings in X. Syria, exhibited{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beside the door to the small staircase (see below).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The door in the end-wall opens upon the Small Staircase{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PI. 27), from which doors lead into the Basilica and the Cabinet{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Coins. At the sides of the staircase ascending to the picture-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gallery (p. 104) are marble statues of Venus and Mercury by Jean-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Baptist e Pigalle, from Sanssouci (p. 193).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We pass through the door straight opposite into the show-rooms{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the *Cabinet of Coins (PL 15, 16). The collection itself, the stu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dents' room, and the technical library are accommodated on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sunk-floor. Interested visitors may have access to particular sec¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tions of the collection by personal application to the director.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The collection contains 300,000 coins, comprising 135,000 antique{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(100,000 Greek and 35,000 Roman) pieces.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The first room (15) affords a survey of the development of coining.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Near the entrance is a large glass-case containing money-ingots, the preli¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}minary stage before coined money; to the left, by the wall, are specimens{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the earliest Greek coins, and further on is a collection of Roman coins{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the earliest heavy cast bronze ingots to the late imperial epoch.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Other sections comprise mediaeval and modern European coins, German,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Brandenburg, and Prussian coins, Oriental coins, and coins now current.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Room 16. Medals. Nearest the door are Italian medals of the 15th cent¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ury. In the show-case in the centre are medals of the Hohenzollern family;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}further on come those of the other German princes and of private persons,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with numerous matrices in box and pearwood, lithographic stone, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}slate. Among the medals of other countries, the modern French specimens{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are most worthy of note. In the small case by the window to the left{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are five *Gold medals (rewards of victory, 3rd cent. A.D.) found in Egypt,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with portraits of Alexander the Great, his mother Olympias, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Emp. Caracalla.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The door straight in front leads from Room 16 to the last room{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Italian sculpture (PI. 17, see p. 104), and the door to the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}into the German section. The latter gives upon Anteroom 18, con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}taining a wall-cabinet in which are various smaller works of art.{% endraw %}
