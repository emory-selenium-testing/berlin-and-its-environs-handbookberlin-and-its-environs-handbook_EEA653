{% raw %}66 Section 2. BERLIN. Royal Palace.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The rooms occupied by the imperial family (no admittance), fitted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}up in 1888-89, are on the first floor, overlooking the Schloss-Platz. Those{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Emperor are between Portals I and II, while the apartments of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Empress adjoin them to the W. Above Portal I is the 'Sternen-Saal'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Star Hall; with the 'Elisabeth- Saal' above it on the second floor; see{% endraw %}
{% raw %}plan of the Royal Palace), containing the colours and standards of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Berlin regiments. A purple banner, hoisted on the N. side, indicates{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Emperor's presence.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The exterior of the palace exhibits in its two principal facades,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}both by Schliiter., a pleasing diversity of style, that to the S. being{% endraw %}
{% raw %}distinguished by monumental severity, while that to the N., over¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}looking the Lustgarten (formerly the garden-front), is enlivened with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}light and elegant details. The statues on the balustrade oi the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}latter are modern. They represent Emperor William I. as Jupiter,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Empress Augusta as Juno, Emperor Frederick as Mars, and Empress{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Frederick as Minerva. The Horse Tamers at Portal IV, two large{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bronze groups by Baron Clodt, were presented by the Emperor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nicholas I. of Russia in 1841. — The W. Facade, by Eosander, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its great central portal, built in imitation of the arch of Septimius{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Severus, is colder in style, but highly effective. The two bronze re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}liefs by 0. Lessing (1897) represent Elector Frederick II. as builder{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the castle, and King Frederick I. as builder of the palace.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the memory of the Netherlandish relations of the Great Elec¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tor, Emperor William II. has recently dedicated six bronze statues.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In front of the 'Schloss-Apotheke' a statue of Admiral Coligny,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Count Gbrtz-Schlitz, was unveiled in 1905 (Coligny, murdered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the Night of St. Bartholomew in 1572, was the great-grandfather{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Louise Henrietta, the first wife of the Great Elector). Five sta¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tues of members of the Orange family on the parapet of the palace-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}terrace followed in 1907: William I. 'the Silent (1533-84), by Schott;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Maurice (1567-1625), by Wolff; Frederick Henry (father of Louise{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Henrietta; 1584-1647), by Briitt; William II. (1626-50), by Haver-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}kamp; and William III. (1650-1702; King of England from 1689),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Baucke.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}By Portal IV of the N. facade, which is opened by a sentry, we{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enter the Outer Court, in the centre of which is a large bronze{% endraw %}
{% raw %}group of St. George and the Dragon, by Kiss (1865). A passage{% endraw %}
{% raw %}leads hence to the *Inner Court, which is considered one of Schlii-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ter's masterpieces; it is surrounded on three sides by arcades. To{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left, in Portal V, is a Statue of the Great Elector, by Franz du{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sart (1651).{% endraw %}

{% raw %}left. The entrance is in the E. wing. Sticks and umbrellas must{% endraw %}
