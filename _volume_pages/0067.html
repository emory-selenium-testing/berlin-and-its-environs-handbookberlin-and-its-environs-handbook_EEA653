{% raw %}To Frederick William, the 'Great Elector'(1640-88), the found¬{% endraw %}

{% raw %}on this side of the town nearly as far as the Spree was now removed,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and on its site was planted a double avenue of lime-trees, on each{% endraw %}
{% raw %}side of which gradually sprang up the handsome modern street{% endraw %}
{% raw %}named Unter den Linden (p. 55). Owing to the introduction of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}foreign settlers, particularly of French Protestant refugees (after{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Revocation of the Edict of Nantes in 1685), the population of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the town increased to 20,000. It now became the seat of an inde¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pendent industrial activity, while the Court zealously promoted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}artistic enterprise and strove to embellish the town, chiefly with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the aid of Dutch architects. The nucleus of the royal library and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}art-collections was also formed at this period.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Frederick III. (1688-1713), who became King Frederick I. in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1701, erected the Friedrichstadt, constituted Berlin a royal resid¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ence, and united the administration of the five quarters of the city.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In 1694 he founded the Academy of Art, and in 1700 the Academy of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Science (p. 57), the first president of the latter being the celebrated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Leibnitz; while in the province of architecture he was fortunate{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in obtaining the services of Andreas Schliiter (b. at Hamburg in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1664, d. at St. Petersburg in 1714), the greatest artist of his period.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}First employed as a sculptor on the Lange Briicke, in the Royal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Palace (p. 65), and on the Arsenal (p. 60), begun by J. A. Nering{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(d. 1695), Schliiter afterwards erected the Palace of Charlot-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tenbnrg (p. 182), and in 1699 began the reconstruction of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Royal Palace, which was not completed until long after his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}death. He was also the sculptor of the Equestrian Statue of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Great Elector (p. 70) in 1703. In 1710 the population, which had{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been steadily augmented by French and Walloon immigrants,{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Friedrichstadt and the Dorotheenstadt, began to pull down the fort¬{% endraw %}
