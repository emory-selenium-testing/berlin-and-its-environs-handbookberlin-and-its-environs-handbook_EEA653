{% raw %}The *Pergamon Museum (PL R, 23), an unpretending edi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fice by F. Wolff, opened in 1901, contains the larger objects found{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the excavations undertaken by the Royal Museums in three Hel¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lenistic towns of Asia Minor, viz. Pergamon (1878-86), Magnesia{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on theMseander (1891-93), and Priene (1895-99). Its chief treasure is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the great **Frieze, representing the contest of the gods and giants{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Gigantomachia), which formed the artistic decoration of a huge{% endraw %}
{% raw %}marble altar to Zeus (and perhaps Athena) on the Acropolis of Per¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gamon, probably erected by King Eumenes II. about the year B.C.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}180 in honour of his decisive victory over the Gauls of Asia Minor.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The altar has been reconstructed so as to exhibit the sculptures in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their original position. The engineer Karl Humauri (d. 1896) discov¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ered several isolated reliefs in 1871 built into a mediaeval forti¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fication wall, and in 1878-80 succeeded in bringing to light the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whole of this most valuable work of art, which is the largest existing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monument of Greek sculpture and rivals in importance the Parthenon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sculptures in the British Museum. The sculptures are characterized{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by strong dramatic feeling and great boldness in the representation{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of scenes of excitement; and at the same time reveal a knowledge{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the human form, a richness of fancy, and a mastery of execution,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}such as are displayed in no other antique remains on so large a scale.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Altar, reconstructed in its original size and shape, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}surrounded by a broad gangway, occupies the centre of the mu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seum. It has the form of a rectangular podium or platform, 30 ft.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in height, and is 113 ft. broad and 124 ft. in length at its base.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A flight of steps, 65 ft. broad (see below), let into the front,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ascends to the top. The opening on the same side, with columns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(a modern arrangement) and preceded by a fine Mosaic Pavement{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from Pergamon, forms the entrance to the inner court (see p. 84).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Round the outside of the altar (including the side-walls of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}flight of steps), above a kind of dado or pedestal, 7lf% ft. in height,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}runs the famous Marble Frieze, also l1/^ ft. high. The high reliefs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have been in many cases reconstructed from numerous small frag¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ments, but none has been restored. Above the frieze are the names{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Gods and Titans, and of the Giants, many of whom are re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}presented in half human, half animal form.{% endraw %}
