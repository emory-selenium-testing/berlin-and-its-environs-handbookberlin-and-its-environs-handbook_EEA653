{% raw %}We now skirt the W. portion of the park of Sanssouci (generally{% endraw %}
{% raw %}closed to the public in summer and autumn) in order to visit the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}New Palace, which lies 1 M. from the Orangery, about l*/4 M. from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Charlottenhof, and 1fi M. from Wildpark Station. The —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*Mew Palace, the summer-residence of the Emperor, was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}planned by Frederick the Great during the Seven Years' War as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a visible proof of his power and wealth and was built by him in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1763-69 at a cost of 450,000^. The plans were furnished by J. G.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Biiring of Hamburg, who followed Dutch and English models.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The main building is 700 ft. in length, and the central portion, sur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mounted by a dome, has three stories. The chief fagade is turned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}towards the E., overlooking the park. To the W., behind the palace,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are the Communs, intended as quarters for the royal retinue,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}picturesquely designed buildings by K. von Gontard, united by a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}semicircular colonnade. The palace and the communs together form{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an imposing pile, though the execution of the details is sometimes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}careless. The sculptures are mediocre, but the effect of the weathered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sandstone figures against the red brick background is very pleasing.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Emperor Frederick III., who was born in the palace in 1831, resided{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in it for a considerable time as crown-prince, and died there in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}June 1888. William II. has thoroughly restored the building and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has added a large terrace, with candelabra by Schott, on the side{% endraw %}
{% raw %}next the garden. Adm., see p. 189; entrance in the S. wing.{% endraw %}
