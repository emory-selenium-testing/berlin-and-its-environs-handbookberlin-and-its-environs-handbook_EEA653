{% raw %}Neither the expansion of the town in the 18th century, nor the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}system of building adopted in 1860, was conducive to originality{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or variety in the different quarters. For miles the whole ground{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was systematically marked out, without any great consideration of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the characteristic difference between the wide main arteries and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}such smaller side-streets as might be found necessary. The enormous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prices of the large building-lots, which were generally very narrow{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in proportion to their depth, necessitated the building of high houses{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with narrow courts. All over the town we find on the same plots{% endraw %}
{% raw %}expensive residences in front and cheaper ones behind, thus causing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a great mixture of all classes of the inhabitants, and great mono¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tony of street effects. It is only within recent years that some of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the suburbs have been laid out on the villa-system.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Just as in London, Paris, and other capitals, so in Berlin, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}upper classes tend on the whole to live in the W. end of the town,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}while the E. end is given over to factories and workshops. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}South-Eastern Quarter is the seat of the more skilled industries,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}such as cabinet-making and the manufacture of articles in bronze{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and other metals. The place of the old building-yards and factories{% endraw %}
