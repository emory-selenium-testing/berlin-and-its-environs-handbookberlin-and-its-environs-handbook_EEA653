{% raw %}The Northern Quarter was from 1860 to 1880 the seat of great{% endraw %}
{% raw %}machine works and foundries. Since then the manufactories have been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}transferred to the N.W. as far as the neighbourhood of Tegel, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}buildings containing the Physical Science Schools and their collec¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tions now stand on the site of the old royal iron-foundry. In the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}extreme N. are the suburbs of Pankow and Nieder-Schonhausen.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— The North-West Quarter is being given over more and more{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to barracks, courts-of-law, medical institutes, and hospitals. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}district of Moabit is surrounded by them, while the Hansa{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Quarter (p. 176), which lies beyond the Spree and adjoins the park{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Schloss Bellevue, can boast of several streets of high-class{% endraw %}
{% raw %}residences.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Western Quarter is the favourite residence of the well-to-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}do inhabitants on account of its proximity to the Tiergarten. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}place of the large park and small villas which once surrounded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the woods, the aristocratic Tiergarten Quarter has arisen since{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1850, with its handsome villas, gardens, and private roads, stretch¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing on the S. to the Landwehr Canal and on the W. to the Zoological{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Garden. The gardens, however, are gradually disappearing before{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the encroachments of bricks and mortar, the ground to the S. of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}canal being almost entirely built over. In the Potsdamer-Strasse{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the business life of the Leipziger-Strasse extends as far as Schone¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}berg. The Kurfiirsten-Damm, a magnificent street beginning on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. side of the Zoological Garden, runs to the S.W. to the Grune¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wald. The surroundings of the Grunewald (p. 184), which marches{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with Halensee, are given over to villas. To the N. the W. end of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Berlin borders on Charlottenburg, to the S. on Schoneberg and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Wilmersdorf, the space once intervening between the city and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}these suburbs being now entirely built over.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Almost every part of Berlin offers a pleasing picture. Its streets{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enjoy a model cleanliness, and a system of main drainage, radiating{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in twelve directions, carries off all its sewage to distant fields.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}There are few dark lanes or alleys even in the old part of the city.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nearly all the newer houses have balconies, gay in summer with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}flowers and foliage. The public squares are embellished with gar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dens, monuments, and fountains, and the newer churches also are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}generally surrounded by small pleasure-grounds. Numerous bridges{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are beautified-by sculpture. The centres of traffic, such as the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Jannowitz-Brucke, the Trebbiner-Strasse, the Lehrte Station, etc.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with their network of railway-lines, and the navigation on the river,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}offer scenes of remarkable animation.{% endraw %}
