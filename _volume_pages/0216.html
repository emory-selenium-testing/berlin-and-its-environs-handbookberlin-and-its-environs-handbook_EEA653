{% raw %}From the Brandenburger Tor the Charlottenburg Chaussee runs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}through the middle of the Tiergarten to Charlottenburg, crossing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Sieges-Allee (p. 142) and the Grosse Stern. Beyond the Sieges-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Allee to the right is a bronze figure of the Victor, by Wand-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}schneider. The Grosse Stern (PI. R, 13), a circular space whence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}roads radiate in all directions, was embellished in 1904 by order of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Emp. William II. with five bronze groups of hunting-scenes, viz.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Hubertus fountain, by K. von Uechtritz; Ancient German buffa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lo-hunt, by Schaper; Mediaeval boar-hunt, by K. Begus;Hare-hunt{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Haverkamp.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the N. margin of the Tiergarten, about 1/2 M. to the W. of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Alsen-Platz (p. 140), on the Spree, are the places of recreation{% endraw %}
{% raw %}known as the Zelte (i. e. tents, from their original construction).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}These are simply 'al fresco' restaurants and have been popular{% endraw %}
{% raw %}resorts ever since the reign of Frederick the Great. The Kron-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}priuzen-Zelt is a handsome building by Grisebach (1888).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the Kurfiirsten-Platz (PI. R, 17), at the W. end of the Zelte,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shady walks traverse the N. part of the Tiergarten, running towards{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the S.E., the S., and the W. In the latter direction, towards the Spree,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the royal Chateau of Bellevue (PI. R, 14), built in 1785 by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Prince Ferdinand of Prussia, the youngest brother of Frederick the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Great (adm., see p. 36). The park (open till dusk) contains a monu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment, by Zumbusch, to Prince Augustus of Prussia (A. 1843),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reorganizer of the Prussian artillery.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Luther-Briicke here crosses the Spree to Moabit (p. 165).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Farther along the river are the Bellevue Station of the Stadtbahn{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PI. R, 14) and the fine residential quarter known as the Hansa{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Quarter (PI. R, 14, 10, 11; comp. p. 54). In the centre, at Klop-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stock-Str. 19-20J is the Imperial Public Health Office (PI. R, 14),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a Romanesque brick edifice by Busse (1896-97). To the S., on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}edge of the Tiergarten, is the Emperor Frederick Memorial{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Church (PI. R, 13), by Vollmer (1895). Adjoining is the popular{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Charlottenhof Restuurunt (p. 10).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The S. margin of the Tiergarten is skirted by the Lenne-Strasse{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the Tiergarten-Strasse, both with tasteful villas and mansions.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Tiergarten-Str., which begins at the Roland Fountain (p. 143),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}we may notice the Staudt Mansion, at the corner of the Regenten-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Str., built by Rieth, with gorgeous plastic ornamentation by Vogel{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Widemann.{% endraw %}
